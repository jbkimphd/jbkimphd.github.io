About Me

Greetings! My name is J.B. (Joo Baek) Kim, and I am an Assistant Professor of Information & Technology Management at the John H. Sykes College of Business, University of Tampa, located in Tampa, Florida. Before joining UT, I was a faculty member at the Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) in Worcester, Massachusetts.

I earned a Ph.D. degree in Information Systems & Decision Science from Louisiana State University. I also hold master's degrees in Business Administration (with a concentration in Operations Management) from Seoul National University, in Manufacturing Management from Pennsylvania State University, and in Information Systems from the University of Arkansas. Prior to entering academia, I worked at the Samsung Economic Research Institute (SERI) and the Korea Information Society Development Institute (KISDI) in South Korea.

My research interests are focused on the pragmatic use of qualitative/quantitative research methods to examine and effectively use information systems and technology to improve society. Specifically, I am interested in exploring the areas of IT strategy in organizations, the role of IT in business transformation, the use of serious games in business education, gamification in business processes, and organizational technology adoption. With my research, I aim to find practical solutions that help organizations leverage technology to achieve their goals.

Research Interests

My research focuses on the pragmatic application of information systems and technology to improve organizational effectiveness and societal outcomes. I employ both qualitative and quantitative research methods to address complex business and technology challenges.

IT Strategy in Organizations

Examining how organizations can effectively align their IT strategy with business objectives to achieve competitive advantage.

Business Transformation

Investigating the role of information technology in enabling and supporting organizational transformation initiatives.

Serious Games in Education

Exploring the application of serious games and gamification techniques in business education and training contexts.

Gamification in Business

Studying how gamification principles can be applied to business processes to improve engagement and performance.

Technology Adoption

Analyzing factors that influence organizational technology adoption and implementation success.

Research Methods

Developing and applying innovative qualitative and quantitative research methodologies in information systems research.
